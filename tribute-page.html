Superman

The Man of Steel

My Personal Tribute

The first superhero I met was Superman. I did not read comics, but at the age of ten, I saw the 1978 movie starring Christopher Reeve. What a hero he was for a young girl! He was even stronger than my daddy, he was smart because he wrote for a newspaper, he used his powers for good, and he was handsome! Whenever the term "swept off of your feet" is used, that visual of Lois Lane being literally lifted into Superman's arms comes to mind.

Superman was created in the minds of writer Jerry Siegal and artist Joe Shuster. Their first Superman comic book was published in 1938, a full 30 years before I was born. It has been one of the best-selling comic books in history with a peak in sales around the mid-40s, around the time my parents were born. It is interesting to think it possible that my grandparents could have been Superman fans in the early years.
